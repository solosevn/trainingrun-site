What is the Weekly News Download?

News Window

We only cover news that occurred within a specific 7-day window:

Approved Sources

We only accept stories from these vetted sources:

The 3-Tier Truth Filter

Every news story must pass through our 3-tier verification system before inclusion. Each tier serves as a discrete gate with its own checklist.

Tier 1 - Source & Provenance Check (Pass/Fail)

This tier is a hard gate. Stories that fail Tier 1 are rejected entirely.

  • Only accept stories from "Approved News Sources" plus clearly labeled primary sources (SEC filings, official company blogs, peer-reviewed papers, court filings, government data)
  • Require at least one primary source or a wire (AP/Reuters) for any market-moving or valuation-sensitive headline
  • Reject: anonymous sources, single-source scoops without corroboration, opinion columns, or posts that primarily frame an argument

Tier 2 - Multi-Source Fact Confirmation (Scored)

For each headline that passes Tier 1, we verify the facts:

  • List 3-5 atomic factual claims (who/what/when/where) for each candidate headline
  • Require at least two independent sources for each critical claim (e.g., AP + SEC filing, or Bloomberg + company blog)
  • Mark any claim that cannot be cross-confirmed as "unverified" and either (a) drop the story, or (b) include the uncertainty explicitly in the wording ("reportedly", "according to X, not yet confirmed by Y")

Tier 3 - Framing, Bias, and Opinion Stripping (Scored)

The final tier ensures neutral, fact-first presentation:

  • Rewrite every headline in neutral, data-first language that a hostile fact-checker would accept
  • Remove adjectives that imply value judgments ("huge", "shocking", "game-changing") unless they are quotes and you attribute them
  • Verify that any number in the headline (dates, valuations, GW, percentages) is explicitly backed by your Tier-2 claim list and sources

Truth Score Formula

The Truth Score is a transparent 0-100 calculation that measures the verification strength of each headline. We publish this rubric so viewers can audit our work.

Tier 1 (Source Integrity) - 40 Points

40 points Primary document + at least one top-tier newswire (AP/Reuters) or equivalent
30 points Two or more reputable outlets, no primary doc
0 points Fails Tier 1 - story rejected (never scored)

Tier 2 (Fact Confirmation) - 40 Points

  • Start at 40 points
  • Subtract 5-10 points for each material fact that is single-sourced or disputed across outlets
  • Subtract if any detail must be reported as "unconfirmed" or "reportedly"

Tier 3 (Framing and Neutrality) - 20 Points

20 points Headline and segment copy contain zero subjective language, and all numbers appear in claim sheet with sources
10 points Minor soft language or slight extrapolation
0 points Heavily opinionated framing (story should be rewritten or rejected)

Total: 0-100 Truth Score

Formula: Tier 1 (up to 40) + Tier 2 (up to 40) + Tier 3 (up to 20) = Truth Score

Truth Score Badges

Each headline receives a badge based on its Truth Score:

90-100: VERIFIED Highest confidence - primary sources + full corroboration + neutral framing
80-89: CONFIRMED Strong confidence - solid sourcing with minor gaps
70-79: SOLID Acceptable confidence - meets minimum bar for inclusion
Below 70: NOT FEATURED Does not meet our standards - story is not included

Weekly Funnel and Ranking Process

Here's how we go from hundreds of potential stories to the top 3 featured on the show:

Step 1: Start Wide

Pull 20-30 AI-relevant headlines in your time window (Thu 00:01-Wed 23:59).

Step 2: Apply Hard Rejection Rules Before Scoring

  • Outside AI/tech or clearly tangential = Rejected
  • No primary or Tier-1-approved source = Rejected
  • Heavy speculation (rumors of deals, unnamed sources) = Rejected

Step 3: Score Remaining Headlines

Score the remaining set with the Truth Score and add a separate Impact Score (0-100) based on:

  • Breadth:strong> How many people/companies affected
  • Structural importance: Benchmarks, regulation, infrastructure
  • Time-sensitivity: Does it materially change the landscape this week?

Step 4: Select Final Headlines

  • Top 10 by Impact Score (only among stories with Truth Score >= 80)
  • From those, feature the top 3 on the show
  • List the rest on the website under "This Week's Verified Headlines"

This keeps the on-air segment tight but shows viewers the wider funnel and reinforces that we're not cherry-picking.
