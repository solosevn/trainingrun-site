TRUscore Methodology

TRUscore is an independent data axis measuring AI truthfulness, neutrality, and factual reliability. Unlike TRS (which measures overall capability), TRUscore measures whether a model can be trusted to tell you the truth. This page documents our complete methodology with full source citations.

Last Updated: March 3, 2026 — V1.4

Why TRUscore Exists

AI models are increasingly used to inform decisions, shape opinions, and deliver information to billions of people. The people behind the keys — the engineers, the companies, the institutions — should not be using AI to further their own ideological views. TRUscore exists to hold AI models accountable to one standard: verifiable truth.

Truth has no ideology. Truth has no party. Truth is verifiable or it isn’t truth.

The Five Pillars

TRUscore = Truthfulness (35%) + Confabulation (20%) + Reasoning (20%) + Neutrality (15%) + Response Quality (10%)
Pillar Weight Why This Weight
Truthfulness 35% Getting facts right is the core of trust. Everything else is secondary if the model cannot answer correctly.
Confabulation 20% A distinct failure mode — the model isn’t biased, it’s reconstructing plausible but false outputs from patterns in its training data. Catastrophic when it happens.
Reasoning 20% A truthful model must also reason correctly. Hard logical and scientific questions reveal whether accuracy extends beyond memorised facts.
Neutrality 15% Bias is a distinct threat to truth. A model can be factually correct but systematically distort reality through ideological framing.
Response Quality 10% Truthfulness degrades if responses are unhelpful, evasive, or refuse to engage. Real-world human preference validates that accuracy lands.

What Is Confabulation?

Confabulation is the production of fabricated, distorted, or misinterpreted information — without any conscious intent to deceive. The term comes from neuropsychology, where it describes patients who unconsciously invent memories to fill gaps, genuinely believing them to be true.

Unlike “hallucination” — which implies seeing things that aren’t there — confabulation precisely captures what language models do: reconstruct plausible-sounding outputs from patterns in training data. The model isn’t malfunctioning; it’s doing exactly what it was built to do, just without the facts to back it up.

“It ought to be called confabulation, not hallucination… We make up a memory when we need it. That’s exactly what the chatbots do.”
— Geoffrey Hinton, Naked Scientists, June 2024

Sources: APA Dictionary of Psychology • Merriam-Webster • Geoffrey Hinton, Naked Scientists (June 2024)

Pillar 1: Truthfulness (35%)

SimpleQA — 13%

4,326 fact-seeking questions graded as Correct / Incorrect / Not Attempted. Created by OpenAI as an independent benchmark. 42+ models scored. Tests hard factual accuracy on short-form questions.

kaggle.com/benchmarks/openai/simpleqa

FACTS Benchmark Suite — 12%

Google DeepMind’s factual accuracy benchmark across four domains: Parametric (internal knowledge), Search (retrieval accuracy), Multimodal (image-grounded facts), and Grounding (context fidelity). Average score used. 15+ frontier models. Independently reproduced by Kaggle.

kaggle.com/benchmarks/google/facts

TruthfulQA — 10%

817 questions designed to test whether AI models avoid common misconceptions and widely-held false beliefs. Evaluated on MC1 + MC2 formats, scores averaged. Maintained at llm-stats.com with 100+ models scored continuously.

llm-stats.com/benchmarks/truthfulqa

Pillar 2: Confabulation (20%)

HalluHard — 12%

Multi-turn confabulation benchmark across four high-stakes domains: Legal Cases, Research Questions, Medical Guidelines, and Coding. Designed with deliberately hard and ambiguous prompts to stress-test frontier models. Score = confabulation rate (lower is better — inverted during normalization). Web-search-assisted variants excluded so only base model capability is measured.

halluhard.com

Vectara Confabulation Leaderboard — 8%

Confabulation percentage rates for 30+ models tested across 7,700+ articles in 7 domains (news, science, medicine, legal, sports, business, education). Uses HHEM-2.3 evaluation model. The industry standard for confabulation measurement at scale.

huggingface.co/spaces/vectara/leaderboard

Pillar 3: Reasoning (20%)

Humanity’s Last Exam (HLE) — 10%

2,500 expert-level questions across mathematics, science, law, and humanities — contributed by domain specialists worldwide. Designed to be unsolvable by pattern matching alone. Tests deep reasoning at the absolute frontier of model capability. Published by the Center for AI Safety.

lastexam.ai

LiveBench Reasoning — 10%

Contamination-free benchmark updated monthly with new questions drawn from recent papers, news, and datasets. Cannot be memorised from training data — questions are always new. Global Average score used. 100+ models on HuggingFace.

livebench.ai

Pillar 4: Neutrality (15%)

Anthropic Paired Prompts Evaluation — 15%

1,350 prompt pairs across 150 political topics, each tested from two opposing ideological perspectives. Measures even-handedness percentage — does the model respond consistently regardless of which side is asking? Methodology open-sourced on GitHub.

anthropic.com/news/political-even-handedness

Pillar 5: Response Quality (10%)

TS Arena — 10%

Head-to-head human preference arena evaluating AI safety and helpfulness side-by-side. Real users compare model outputs and vote for the response that is both safe and genuinely useful. Penalises over-refusal and evasion alongside harmful outputs. Operated by Training Run.

tsarena.ai

Complete TRUscore Formula — V1.4

TRUscore V1.4 =
  Truthfulness: (SimpleQA × 0.13) + (FACTS × 0.12) + (TruthfulQA × 0.10)
  Confabulation: (HalluHard × 0.12) + (Vectara × 0.08)
  Reasoning: (HLE × 0.10) + (LiveBench × 0.10)
  Neutrality: (Anthropic × 0.15)
  Response Quality: (TSArena × 0.10)

All scores normalized 0–100. Confabulation rates inverted (lower rate = higher score). Null sources excluded; remaining weights renormalized proportionally. Minimum 3 of 9 sub-metrics required to qualify for the leaderboard. V1.4: 9 sub-metrics across 5 pillars.
